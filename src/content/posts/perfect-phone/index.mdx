I remember when the Nintendo Switch was announced, I thought it was very clever to have a console that could be used as both a handheld and a home console.

When the Steam Deck was announced, I saw it as the next big step: a device with the same versatility as the Nintendo Switch, but not limited to gaming.
It can also be used as a desktop computer: with a USB-C hub, you can connect a keyboard, mouse, multiple monitors, ethernet, speakers, etc.
With the openness of Linux under the hood, you can turn the Steam Deck into anything you want.

In parallel to these devices, phones have slowly evolved into our everyday devices.
With each generation, they get more powerful and more capable.
And yet I feel like we're not using them to their full potential.

Let's forget for a moment about our preconceived notions about phones and imagine what a portable device with such capabilities and form factor could be.

## The computing brick

You are looking at a 73.0&nbsp;mm x 154&nbsp;mm x 9.0&nbsp;mm slab.

It weights around 200&nbsp;g.
On the front, there is a 6.3" display with a 20:9 aspect ratio.

 

## Things I cannot compromise on

If any of these features are missing, I'm not even considering buying the phone:

- OLED or another display technology that offers deep blacks
- 1080p resolution or higher
- Sustained brightness of 700&nbsp;nits or higher
- Stereo speakers
- USB-C port
- SIM tray
- 6.0" screen size or larger

## Important features

These are the features that--if missing--would likely dissuade me from buying the phone:

- 5G capable
- Headphone jack
- Wi-Fi 6 or newer
- Bluetooth 5.2 or newer
- 5 000&nbsp;mAh capacity or higher
- 120&nbsp;Hz refresh rate or higher
- MicroSD storage expansion slot
- 8&nbsp;GB RAM or higher
- 500 000 Antutu (v11) score or higher
- IP68 or higher water resistance
- Average photo and video quality or better

## Nice to have

These are the features I would like to see in a phone:

- No camera bump
- Variable refresh rate down to 1Hz
- Replaceable battery, screen, ports, back panel
- IR emitter
- NFC
- Wireless charging
- eSIM support
- No notch or hole-punch
- Physical switch for disabling cameras and microphones
- Alternative OS support
- aptX Adaptive / Snapdragon Sound support
